_ how I work

I believe in honest design and meaningful development—clear, flexible systems that grow with the product. I like working closely with teams, moving fast without breaking what's important, and building things that solve real problems for real people.

Some principles I try to stick to:

  • Form follows function (but doesn't forget delight)
  • Be intentional, or don't bother
  • Small details are never small
  • Build systems, not silos
  • Ask why before how

* a few things I love

Outside of work, you'll probably find me experimenting with motion, collecting type specimens, or sketching ideas I may never ship. I like tools that get out of the way, interfaces that feel invisible, and side projects that go nowhere (but teach me everything).

When I’m not designing or coding, you’ll likely catch me playing guitar, singing (or at least trying to), or chasing tennis balls on the court. Music and movement keep me balanced—and oddly enough, they teach me a lot about rhythm in design, too.
