Mobile App Audit & Redesign Proposal

A detailed audit of the current mobile app UI, identifying usability and design issues, followed by a redesign proposal aimed at improving user experience and aesthetics.

App name:

YourHealthyDay

Industry:

Health, Well-being

Timeline:

6 weeks

Tech stack:

FullStory, Figma

Overview

Redesigned the app's functionalities to resolve user pain points and enhance user flows

Original design

Redesign

Insights

Problem 1

Users can only transfer the same amount to all payees in a single transaction

This limitation hindered users from making individualized payments to multiple recipients, necessitating separate transactions instead.

Solution 1

Allow users to pay varying amounts to multiple payees in a single transaction

This helps eradicate the necessity for individual transfers, thereby enhancing efficiency.

Problem 2

Users only can add one bank account to the app

This reduced the flexibility for users to select from various accounts. For instance, users might desire to utilize one account for travel-related purchases and another for general expenses.

Solution 2

Allow users to add multiple bank accounts and easily switch between them

Security considerations, like implementing 2-factor authentication, are factored in when users link additional bank accounts.

Problem 3

Current transaction history does not provide information about users' financial patterns

This resulted in a lack of insights into their spending habits, making it hard for individuals to track expenses, identify overspending, or manage finances effectively.

Solution 3

Introduce a new expense-tracking feature that provides users with insights into their spending habits

Users have the capability to view their expenses categorized by different segments over various time frames. This feature aims to empower users in making more informed financial decisions.

Process

User Flow

Ideating solutions to solve pain points and generating process flow

I interviewed four frequent app users across diverse demographics to pinpoint their pain points, brainstorm potential solutions, and create a comprehensive process flow to help me understand the sequence of actions that the users undertake to reach their goals.

Prototyping

Translating wireframes to designs and prototypes

Starting with wireframes helped organize the app's structure, followed by developing UI components to design high-fidelity mockups. Subsequently, I progressed by prototyping solutions tailored to address each identified user pain point.

Usability Testing

Revamping the solutions after user feedback

I presented Figma prototypes to five users to collect valuable feedback, assessing the effectiveness and usability of the proposed solutions. Most users found the new features beneficial, although there were some minor issues with the payment screen.

Reflections

Takeaways

Utilizing various user research and testing methods to inform design decisions

I chose interviews and usability testing as my user research techniques due to their suitability for a small user group. In the future, I hope to include surveys, competitive analysis, and A/B testing to gain more comprehensive insights for better design decisions.

Conducting detailed user research focused on specific sections of the app

If given more time, I hope to conduct focused interviews with more users for specific sections of the app, such as the home and payment screens, to derive more detailed and tailored user pain points.
